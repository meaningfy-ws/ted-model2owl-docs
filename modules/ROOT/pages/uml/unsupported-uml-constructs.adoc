[[sec:unsupported-uml-constructs]]
== Unsupported UML Constructs

model2owl transforms a deliberately constrained *subset* of UML class-diagram
constructs into OWL ontologies and SHACL shapes. UML offers several modelling
constructs whose semantics either have no direct counterpart in the target
formalisms or are intentionally out of scope. This section lists those
constructs, explains what each one is, and states what model2owl does with it
(or does instead).

Modellers should avoid relying on these constructs to carry semantics that they
expect to appear in the generated artefacts. Where a richer relationship is
required, it should be expressed with a supported construct (see the
xref:uml/conv-connectors.adoc#sec:connectors[Connector conventions]) or captured
out-of-band.

[[sec:unsupported-nary-associations]]
=== N-ary (ternary and higher-arity) associations

An *n-ary association* is a single association that relates three or more
classifiers at once (a ternary association relates three, and so on), typically
drawn as a diamond connecting all participating ends. Only *binary*
associations — those relating exactly two classes — are transformed. N-ary
associations (arity greater than two) are *ignored* in the generated RDF
artefacts: no object properties, classes, or shapes are produced for them.

To represent an n-ary relationship, *reify* it: introduce an intermediate class
that stands for the relationship and connect it to each participant with a
binary association. See the supported connector set in
xref:uml/conv-general.adoc#rule:gen-model-connectors[Supported UML connectors].

[[sec:unsupported-qualified-associations]]
=== Qualified associations

A *qualified association* attaches a *qualifier* (one or more attributes) to an
association end, partitioning the set of associated instances by the qualifier
value — conceptually similar to a keyed lookup. model2owl does *not* represent
the qualifier: the association is not transformed as qualified, and the
qualifier carries no meaning in the generated artefacts. Model the relationship
as a plain binary association, and express any key-like semantics through an
explicit attribute or a dedicated class.

[[sec:unsupported-aggregation-composition]]
=== Aggregation and composition associations

UML distinguishes plain associations from *shared aggregation* (hollow diamond)
and *composite aggregation / composition* (filled diamond), which add
part-whole semantics — and, for composition, lifecycle dependency of the part
on the whole. model2owl treats both shared and composite aggregation as a
*plain association*: the part-whole semantics are *not* generated (no
distinguished part-of/has-part axioms, no lifecycle constraints). If part-whole
meaning must be conveyed, encode it explicitly through suitably named and typed
object properties rather than relying on the aggregation adornment.

[[sec:unsupported-covering-constraints]]
=== Covering and completeness constraints on generalization sets

A *generalization set* groups several generalizations sharing one superclass and
may carry constraints. The `{complete}` (covering) constraint asserts that every
instance of the superclass belongs to at least one of the listed subclasses,
while the `{disjoint}` constraint asserts that no instance belongs to more than
one. model2owl does *not* transform these generalization-set constraints:
`{complete}`/covering constraints are *not* turned into OWL covering axioms
(`owl:unionOf` / covering `owl:equivalentClass` axioms over the subclasses), and
`{disjoint}` constraints are *not* turned into `owl:disjointWith` /
`owl:AllDisjointClasses` axioms. Note that covering (completeness) and
disjointness are independent constraints: each may hold without the other. If
either axiom is required in the ontology, it must be added by other means.
